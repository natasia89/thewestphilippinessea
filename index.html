The West Philippine Sea (WPS)

The West Philippine Sea (WPS) is the name used by the Philippine government for portions of the South China Sea that fall within the Philippines’ maritime zones, including areas inside its 200-nautical-mile Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ).

Important Areas

  • Scarborough Shoal (Bajo de Masinloc / Panatag Shoal)
  • Parts of the Spratly Islands (Kalayaan Island Group)

Legal Basis

1. Philippines v. China (2016 Arbitration Ruling)

  • China’s Nine-Dash Line claim has no legal basis under international law.
  • Areas within 200 nautical miles belong to the Philippine EEZ.
  • China violated Philippine sovereign rights by interfering with fishing and exploration.

2. United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS)

  • Grants sovereign rights within a 200-nautical-mile EEZ.
  • West Philippine Sea areas fall within the Philippine EEZ.

3. Philippine Domestic Law

  • Republic Act No. 9522 (Archipelagic Baselines Law)
  • Official naming in 2012 under President Benigno Aquino III

Natural Resources

  • Major fishing grounds supporting millions of Filipino fishermen
  • Malampaya gas field
  • Potential oil and gas reserves in Reed Bank
  • Rich coral reefs and marine biodiversity
  • One of the world's busiest global trade routes

Claimant Countries

  • Philippines
  • China
  • Vietnam
  • Malaysia
  • Brunei
  • Taiwan

Current Developments

  • Artificial island construction in disputed areas
  • Military facilities established in contested regions
  • Diplomatic protests and patrol operations by the Philippines

Summary

The West Philippine Sea forms part of the Philippines’ 200-nautical-mile EEZ and is legally supported by UNCLOS and the 2016 Arbitration Ruling. It contains valuable marine, oil, and gas resources and remains a significant area of geopolitical importance.
